Intelligent Perception and Autonomous Optimization Laboratory (IPAOLab)

Zhang Hengmin, PhD, Professor, PhD Supervisor, and a recipient of the National High-Level Young Talents Program. He obtained his bachelor’s and master’s degrees in Mathematics, and later earned his Doctor of Engineering degree in Control Science and Engineering from the School of Computer Science and Engineering, Nanjing University of Science and Technology. After graduating with his PhD, he first conducted postdoctoral research at the School of Information Science and Engineering, East China University of Science and Technology, and then pursued further postdoctoral research at the Faculty of Science and Technology, University of Macau, and the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Nanyang Technological University.

A New Integrated Intelligence Paradigm of Perception-Inference-Decision-Making in Open Environments

This research project aims to explore a new integrated intelligence paradigm of Perception-Inference-Decision-Making for open environments. In response to the characteristics of multi-modal and few-shot data such as images and videos in complex open scenarios, the research is committed to constructing a novel intelligent framework with the synergistic integration of data-driven and knowledge-guided approaches, and focuses on investigating the synergistic and mutually promoting mechanism of autonomous perception and logical reasoning in dynamic environments, so as to break through the limitations of traditional visual systems that rely on the assumption of closed environments. Ultimately, the objective of the project is to drive intelligent systems to achieve a fundamental leap from constrained perception to open-world cognition, and provide theoretical and technical support for the development of the new generation of artificial intelligence.

New Theories and Methods for Interpretable Visual Computing

This research project focuses on developing new theories and methods for interpretable visual computing. Confronted with the challenges posed by real-world complex scenarios, it aims to break through the limitations of traditional "black-box" prediction. By ingeniously integrating classical mathematical models such as sparse coding, low-rank learning and tensor decomposition with advanced deep neural network architectures, the research endeavors to construct a new generation of visual computing methods that feature strong generalization ability, structural interpretability and continuous evolution characteristics. Its core objective is to drive a fundamental paradigm shift of visual intelligence systems from unpredictable "black boxes" to transparent and interpretable "white boxes", thereby laying a solid theoretical and technical foundation for reliable human-machine collaboration and extensive trustworthy AI applications.

New Mechanisms and Frameworks for Autonomous Optimization of Visual Intelligence

This research project focuses on exploring new mechanisms and frameworks for the autonomous optimization of visual intelligence. Addressing the core challenges of low computational efficiency and the lack of generalization theories prevalent in ultra-large-scale non-convex optimization tasks, it aims to construct a lightweight adaptive optimization theoretical system with rigorous mathematical convergence guarantees, and thereby develop a core optimization engine that enables efficient and autonomous evolution across the entire chain from model training to edge deployment. Its core objective is to lay a solid theoretical foundation and provide critical methodological support for the efficient training and reliable deployment of visual intelligence systems, thus overcoming the underlying optimization bottlenecks and driving the synchronous improvement of the performance and efficiency of visual systems.

These research efforts focus on the directions of intelligent perception and autonomous optimization in visual information processing and analysis, aiming to systematically break through the core theoretical bottlenecks and key technical challenges encountered, and drive the systematic, innovative development of intelligent science. The relevant achievements will gradually form a series of solutions with both demonstrative significance and promotion value in several key fields, including unmanned system perception, industrial visual quality inspection, intelligent medical imaging, bioinformatic computing, dynamic inference networks, and autonomous evolution prediction systems. To date, more than 30 academic papers have been published in international authoritative journals including the IEEE Transactions series (e.g., TPAMI, TIP, TIFS, TNNLS, TCYB, TCSVT), Pattern Recognition, Information Fusion, as well as CCF Class A conferences.
