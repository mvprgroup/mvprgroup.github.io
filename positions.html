Introduction

In response to the major national strategic demands for artificial intelligence, our research group targets the international academic frontier and focuses on the fundamental theoretical innovation, key technological breakthroughs and cutting-edge application research in such directions as pattern recognition, artificial intelligence, computer vision and large-scale optimization. Our main research directions include:

  1. A New Paradigm for Integrated Perception-Inference in Open Environments: For visual big data such as images and videos, we construct a visually cognitive computing framework driven by the synergy of data and knowledge, explore the mechanisms of autonomous perception and inference in open and dynamic scenarios, and realize a fundamental leap from constrained perception to open-world cognition.
  2. New Theories and Methods for Interpretable Visual Computing: By deeply integrating classical mathematical models (e.g., sparse coding, low-rank representation and tensor analysis) with cutting-edge neural network architectures, we develop a new generation of visual computing methods for complex real-world scenarios, which feature strong generalization, interpretability and adaptive evolution capabilities.
  3. New Mechanisms and Frameworks for Autonomous Optimization of Visual Intelligence: Addressing the fundamental challenges of low computational efficiency, insufficient generalization performance and an incomplete theoretical system in ultra-large-scale and highly non-convex optimization, we construct a lightweight and adaptive optimization framework with rigorous theoretical guarantees, and form a core driving force for the full-chain autonomous evolution from model training to system deployment.

Our research achievements aim to systematically tackle the bottlenecks in fundamental theories and key technologies of intelligent perception and autonomous optimization, drive the systematic breakthrough and upgrading of intelligent science, and form demonstrative solutions in nationally strategic emerging fields in urgent need—such as environmental perception, industrial quality inspection, medical imaging and bioinformatics.
